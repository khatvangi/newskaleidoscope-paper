# Table 6: Structural Absences — Clusters Present in One Corpus but Absent from the Other

## A. Present in Iran, Absent from Ukraine

| Emergent Cluster | Articles | % | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Religious & Divine Legitimacy | 15 | 1.0% | Iran's religious-state identity generates sacred framing. Ukraine conflict was never framed in divine terms by any language community. |

| Latin American Sovereignty Critique | 27 | 2.1% | Latin American outlets invoke anti-imperialist tradition against US strikes. No equivalent frame appeared for Russian invasion. |

## B. Present in Ukraine, Absent from Iran

| Emergent Cluster | Articles | % | Interpretation |

| Ukrainian Military Resistance Celebration | 52 | 2.8% | Clear defender enables heroic resistance narrative. Iran strike has no unambiguous defender to celebrate. |

## C. Methodological Note

These absences were not identified by searching for predefined categories. They emerged when the same clustering algorithm, applied to both corpora, produced categories in one but not the other. The absence of a cluster is as empirically grounded as its presence — it means no group of articles in that corpus shared enough semantic similarity to form that category.
