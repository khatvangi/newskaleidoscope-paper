## Frame 1: Operational Mechanics — "How did the trafficking operation function?"

### Quantitative Absence
- Articles asking about recruitment methods, grooming process, operational structure: **1 out of 4,845** (0.02%)
- This means 99.98% of coverage never asks HOW the trafficking operation functioned

### What IS Said Instead
The corpus mentions "trafficking" 56 times in cluster labels, but frames it as a REVELATION (files reveal trafficking) not as a SYSTEM (here's how the pipeline worked). The one article that touches operational mechanics does so only in passing.

### The Structural Question Nobody Asks
"How were victims identified, recruited, transported, and controlled? What was the operational pipeline from first contact to exploitation? Who managed logistics?"

## Frame 3: Institutional Complicity — "Why did universities enable this?"

- The ratio: 1,389 articles name institutions, ZERO discuss the donor vetting system

### What IS Said Instead
Coverage is entirely individual-institution: "Harvard received $9.1M," "MIT Media Lab accepted $850K." Nobody asks: "What is the structural process by which a university development office accepts money from a convicted sex offender?"

### Entity Context Evidence
From 552 entity-article contexts:

### The Structural Question Nobody Asks
"What is the donor vetting process at elite universities? How many checks did Epstein's donations pass through? Who signed off? Has the process changed? Could it happen again?"

### Quantitative Absence

- This is the least-asked question in the entire corpus

### What IS Said Instead
Coverage names people who "flew on the plane" or "visited the island" but almost never distinguishes between social contacts and trafficking consumers. The conflation is itself a framing choice — it lets the media name-drop without accusing.
